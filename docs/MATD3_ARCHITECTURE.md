# Multi-Agent TD3 (MATD3) Architecture

This document describes the architectural implementation of the MATD3 (Multi-Agent Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient) agent applied to the NOMA-based Cognitive Radio Network.

The architecture strictly follows a **Centralized Training with Decentralized Execution (CTDE)** paradigm.

---

## High-Level Architecture Overview

The system consists of three major components:
1. **Decentralized Actors (Secondary Users)**
2. **Centralized Relay (Coordination Agent)**
3. **Centralized Critics (MATD3 Evaluation)**

### 1. Decentralized Actors (Secondary Users)
Each Secondary User (SU) operates as an independent agent handling partial observability due to distributed wireless fading.

*   **Local History Input ($h_t^i$)**: Since instantaneous global Channel State Information (CSI) is not available at each SU, the agent relies on a local observation history $h_t^i$ containing the latest $L$ observations.
*   **GRU Encoder**: The local history $h_t^i$ is processed by a Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU). The GRU condenses the sequential history into a representative **Belief State ($\hat{b}_t^i$)**.
*   **MLP (Actor)**: The Belief State is passed through a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) Actor network, which directly outputs a continuous action $a_t^i$. In this CRN scenario, the action corresponds to the **Transmit Power** of the SU.

### 2. Centralized Relay (Coordination Agent)
The relay acts as a central coordinating agent that aggregates information from all participating SUs to make an optimal relaying decision.

*   **Belief State Aggregation**: The individual belief states ($\hat{b}_t^1, \hat{b}_t^2, \hat{b}_t^3, \dots, \hat{b}_t^N$) generated by the decentralized actors are collected and concatenated into a global belief representation.
*   **Relay MLP**: The concatenated global belief state is passed into the Relay's Multi-Layer Perceptron (Relay MLP).
*   **Relay Action ($a_t^R$)**: The Relay MLP outputs the relay power allocation action $a_t^R$.

### 3. Centralized Critics (MATD3 Evaluation)
During training, Centralized Critics evaluate the performance of the joint policy. These critics are *only* used during training and are discarded during decentralized execution.

*   **Centralized Information Vector**: A state-action vector is constructed by concatenating the global belief state (from the SUs) with the joint actions taken by the SUs ($a_t^1, \dots, a_t^N$) and the Relay ($a_t^R$).
*   **Multi-Objective Q-Networks**: The framework uses three independent Critic networks to estimate different metrics:
    1.  **Critic $Q_{Throughput}$**: Estimates the expected sum-throughput of the Secondary Users ($Q_T$).
    2.  **Critic $Q_{QoS}$**: Estimates the expected Quality of Service (QoS) metric, specifically the Primary User's satisfaction or outage probability ($Q_{QoS}$).
    3.  **Critic $Q_{Energy}$**: Estimates the total expected energy consumption of the Secondary Network ($Q_E$).

### Optimization and Policy Update
The policy is updated using a constrained optimization objective. The primary goal is to maximize Throughput ($Q_T$) subject to Lagrangian constraints on QoS ($Q_{QoS}$) and Energy ($Q_E$). 

The gradients from the Critics are backpropagated through the Relay MLP and the Decentralized Actor MLPs to update the policy weights, ensuring cooperative behavior among all SUs and the Relay.
